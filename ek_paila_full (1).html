The meaning behind Ek Paila

Not everyone has it figured out. Not everyone reaches the end in a straight line. Ek Paila — one step — is just that. A step towards something better. We all came here for a reason, better education, better opportunities, a better future. This campaign is just one step in that direction. Together.

I didn't know what NSAS was when I landed. I figured it out the same way most of you did — by showing up, looking around, and slowly finding my people. I'm not running because I have everything figured out. I'm running because I know exactly what it feels like to be new here, to be grinding, and to still not feel like enough — and I think we can fix that.

01
Who I am & why I'm here
The story behind the campaign

I'm Prasun Devkota — freshman, finance major, second semester. I came in 2025 not knowing a single person in NSAS. My first real introduction to this community was the Teej program. After the event ended, while most people left, I stayed back and helped clean up. That's when Riyag bhai noticed me and suggested I run. That moment tells you everything about how I approach things.

Since then I've been part of the Henna event — watched how the finances work, how reservations are made, how department funding flows, how food and resources get managed. I'm still learning. But I'm a finance major — numbers, planning, and making things sustainable is literally what I'm here to study. And I've been doing real event management since my Nepal Scout days — 5+ years of coordinating people, programs, and logistics on the ground.

People say I'm too young, too new, too inexperienced. But who here walked in knowing everything? Nobody figures it out before they start. They figure it out by starting. I've been inside this community since day one — not watching from the outside, not applying because it looks good on a resume. Because I actually care.

02
What's actually going on
The reality we don't always say out loud

Our big events — Teej, Henna, sports — they're genuinely good. The vibe after, the dancing, the food, being around your own people far from home — that's irreplaceable and we should protect it. But we always miss people. Not because they don't care — because life here is relentless. Class, work, study, repeat. A 5–6 hour event on the wrong day is just too much.

And even when people show up — some of them are still on the edge. Sitting quietly. Waiting for someone to come to them. Feeling like everyone else knows each other and they missed something. Nobody tells you that you don't belong. But sometimes you feel it anyway.

Then there's the comparison. Someone's already got an internship. Someone switched to a better major. Someone seems to have it all figured out. That pressure is real and it affects people more than we talk about — including what classes they choose, what paths they take, how confident they feel walking into a room.

03
What Ek Paila will actually do
Specific. Realistic. No empty promises.
☕
Chiya & Guff — The drop-in meetup
Not chiya and painting — that already exists and it's great. This is different. A monthly no-agenda, no-RSVP, come-whenever gathering — someone's backyard, a common area, wherever. Just tea, just conversation, just people. For the ones who can't make the big events. For the ones who are still finding their feet.
📅
NSAS Calendar — So nobody's caught off guard
A proper, shared calendar of all upcoming events — posted well in advance. No more finding out about things last minute. People can plan around it, tell their managers at work, actually show up.
🎓
Real support for incoming freshmen
When I landed, I had no clue. Class selection, academic paths, how things actually work here — nobody should have to figure that out alone. We'll make sure every new student from Nepal has someone to call who's been through it. Not a pamphlet. A person.
⚽
Female participation in sports — actually fix it
The previous panel did a great job with the sports event. But female engagement was low — and that's not anyone's fault, it's a design problem. We'll restructure sports events so women aren't an afterthought — mixed formats, more inclusive activities, events designed so everyone actually wants to be there.
📱
Social media that actually lives
396 followers, 95 posts — decent base. But we go quiet between events. Daily presence: fun facts about Nepal, quizzes, polls, behind-the-scenes, student spotlights. Not just event announcements. Content that makes people feel like they're part of something even when there's no event happening. And yes — growing our reach helps more students from Nepal choose SELU.
💰
Financial reach — don't just depend on one source
Our seniors built strong relationships with department heads — that funding tradition continues and I'll work to expand it. But we also explore ticketed events, campus grants, small sponsorships, and reasonable entry fees for outsider-facing events. Sustainability, not profit. So we can do more without always worrying if we have enough.
🌏
Open our events — share our culture
Our events are already open to everyone. We just need to actually tell people. Flyers, campus partnerships, cross-org collaborations. More non-Nepali faces at our events means more people understanding our culture — and more eyes on what NSAS really is. Charge a reasonable amount, cover costs, grow the reach.
